# Weekly Decision Brief

## 2. Purpose
- Convert fragmented information into a structured decision brief.
- Provide clarity, not conclusions.

Expected outcome:
- Clear context
- Visible trade-offs
- Explicit risks
- Action-oriented next steps

---

## 3. When to use
- Reviewing weekly notes
- Preparing for decisions
- Synthesizing multiple inputs
- Clarifying ambiguous situations

Appropriate context:
- Information is incomplete
- Signals are mixed
- Decision pressure exists

---

## 4. When NOT to use
- Creative writing tasks
- Brainstorming sessions
- Detailed technical documentation
- Tasks requiring deep domain expertise

Boundaries:
- Does not make final decisions
- Does not generate speculative conclusions

---

## 5. Inputs
Type of information:
- Notes
- Bullet points
- Meeting summaries
- Metrics
- Rough ideas

Level of structure:
- Can be messy
- Does not require formatting
- May contain contradictions

---

## 6. Output Structure (Mandatory)

1. Context Summary  
2. Key Signals  
3. Constraints & Risks  
4. Options (2â€“4 with pros/cons)  
5. Open Questions  
6. Next Steps  

Fixed order must be respected.

---

## 7. Rules
- Do not invent data.
- Separate facts from assumptions.
- Make trade-offs explicit.
- Avoid generic advice.
- If information is insufficient, say so clearly.

Constraints:
- Keep output concise.
- Prioritize clarity over completeness.

---

## 8. Definition of Success
A good result:
- Can be read in under 5 minutes.
- Makes uncertainty visible.
- Clarifies what matters.
- Enables confident next action.

---

## 9. Example (Optional)

Input:
Notes from weekly review. Engagement dropped in one feature. Mixed user feedback. No clear priority.

Expected Output (Excerpt):

Context Summary:
- Engagement decreased 12% over two weeks.
- Feedback is polarized.

Options:
1. Investigate root causes before iterating.
2. Ship quick iteration based on strongest signals.
3. Temporarily deprioritize feature.

Open Questions:
- Is the decline structural or seasonal?
- Do we have segment-level data?
